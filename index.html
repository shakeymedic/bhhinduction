Critical Clinical Guidelines

Red flags and referral pathways for high-risk presentations.

Mandatory Senior Review (ST4+)

The following patients MUST be reviewed/discharged by an ST4 or above:

  • Atraumatic Chest Pain (Age > 30)
  • Abdominal Pain (Age > 70)
  • Fever in children (Age < 1 year)
  • All Re-attenders (Unscheduled return within 72 hrs)

*ECGs can be signed by FY2+, but escalate if unsure.

Radiology Vetting Rules

General Rule: All CT/MRI requests require vetting by the Duty Radiologist (Ext 42374 / 40345).

EXCEPTIONS (No call needed):
  • CT Head: Trauma / Stroke (As per NICE) - Any time.
  • CT KUB: Renal Colic - In hours only.

Red Flags (NICE)

  • Bilateral sciatica
  • Severe/Progressive bilateral leg weakness
  • Difficulty initiating micturition
  • Urinary retention / Overflow incontinence
  • Loss of rectal fullness sensation / Faecal incontinence
  • Saddle anaesthesia (Perianal/Genital)

Management

Emergency MRI is MANDATORY.

Discussion with local spinal services is NOT required to request the MRI, but request form must contain detailed clinical info.

If scan positive: Refer to Neurosurgery immediately.

Symptoms: Sudden severe pain (abdo/back/pelvis), Clammy/sweaty, Increased HR, Low BP, Syncope.

Action:
  • Request CT Aortogram (Arterial Phase).
  • Discuss with Vascular Team at BHH (via Switch).
  • Confirm imaging modality with vascular clinician if unsure.

Presentation: Tearing chest pain radiating to back/neck/jaw. Pulse deficit. Systolic BP difference in arms.

Investigation: CT Angiogram / MRI is the ONLY definitive diagnostic investigation. (CXR/ECG/USS often normal).

Referral: Cardiothoracic team at QE (via Switch). Control BP and HR.

Presentation: Intense pain disproportionate to skin injury. High fever. Swelling/redness. Later: Dark blotches/blisters.

Action (Category 1 Emergency):
  • Sepsis 6 Bundle immediately (IV Abx, Fluids, Lactate).
  • Urgent Senior Review to exclude Necrotising Fasciitis.
  • Refer Urgent General Surgery (Body) or ENT (Neck) or Vascular (Leg Gangrene).

Rule: All women of childbearing age with abdominal pain MUST have a pregnancy test.

Red Flags: Severe pain, peritonism, shoulder tip pain, syncope.

Action: TV Ultrasound is diagnostic. Refer Gynae urgently if positive/unstable.

"If in doubt, explore." Time is testis.

Action:

  • >16 Years (BHH & QE): Stream direct to SAU. Urology on-call.
  • >16 Years (GHH): ED review < 60 mins. Blue light transfer to BHH Urology.
  • <16 Years: Senior Paediatric Surgical Review.

Red Flags: Stridor, vocal changes, unable to swallow, trismus.

Action: Assess for airway signs. Escalate immediately if clinical concern. High risk of hypoxic cardiac arrest.

"Don't Cruise, Don't Bruise."

Rule: All injuries in children < 1 year who do not mobilise need F2F review with a Consultant. Overnight they are admitted to PAU.

Rota & HR Procedures

Sickness Reporting

You must notify the department if you are unable to attend.

1
Email: juniordoctorsickness@uhb.nhs.uk
Include: Name, Number, Grade, Shift, Site, Reason, Return Date.
2
Call: Consultant via Switchboard (08:00-22:30). Overnight, call ED and ask for Reg/Consultant.
3
Return: Complete 'Return to Work' form with Supervisor upon return.

Leave & Swaps

  • System: Submit via Employee Online.
  • Notice: At least 6 weeks in advance.
  • Restrictions: No annual leave on nights/weekends (must swap).
  • Swaps: Must use swap form. Email to a&erotateam@uhb.nhs.uk.
  • Bank Holidays: You are not automatically off. If rostered, you work.

Study Leave & Expenses

1. Applying for Study Leave

  • Supervisor Approval: You must first get approval from your Educational/Clinical Supervisor.
  • Dual Application: Apply on BOTH Employee Online (EOL) and Accent systems. The Trust cannot approve unless submitted on both.
  • Access EOL: Access via the 'E' on the HGS intranet page under "Skills, Training & HR".
  • Timeline: Submit in good time to allow for cover planning.
  • Service: Centralised at Heartlands. Correspondence goes to the Study Leave email.

2. Claiming Expenses

  • Prior Approval: Study leave must be approved by Postgraduate Medical Education before you can claim.
  • Register: You will receive an email to register with Finance Tools (complete Data Capture Form with payroll number).
  • Log On: nww.fintools.uhb.nhs.uk/UHBFT or via 'ME@qehb' link.
  • Create Claim: Click "New Claim" on dashboard.
  • Travel (Car): Select "Travel" > "Course Mileage". Enter postcodes.
  • Fees: Use "Courses Icon". Max per item £500 (split if higher).
  • Receipts: Upload all receipts/proof of attendance as PDF.
  • Submit: Move claim to 'Line Manager' for approval. Claims at 'Originator' level are not processed.

Education & Wellbeing

IMG Extended Induction

All International Medical Graduates (IMGs) / JSDs undergo an extended induction (min. 4 weeks). You are not required to undertake nights until this is completed.

Teaching Schedule

All new juniors attend the initial 4 teaching days.

  • Day 1: Medical Emergencies (Chest Pain, Sepsis, Stroke).
  • Day 2: Paediatrics & Surgical Presentations.
  • Day 3: Major Trauma, Head Injury, Poisoning.
  • Day 4: Women's Health, ENT, Eyes, Minor Injuries.
